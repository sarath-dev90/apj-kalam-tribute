About

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India. Widely respected for his humility, clarity of thought, and dedication to national development, he inspired generations of students to dream, learn, and serve with integrity.

From his work in India’s space and missile programs to his commitment to education and youth empowerment, Dr. Kalam’s life remains a model of disciplined learning and public service.

Scientist

Key contributions to India’s aerospace and strategic technology programs.

Teacher

An enduring mentor figure who encouraged students to pursue knowledge and purpose.

President

Remembered as the “People’s President” for his approachability and values.

Timeline

1931

Born on 15 October in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

1969

Joined ISRO and played a key role in India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

1983

Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).

1998

Played a pivotal role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests.

2002–2007

Served as the 11th President of India, widely known as the “People’s President”.

2015

Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

Achievements

SLV-III (ISRO)

Associated with early launch vehicle development that strengthened India’s space capability.

IGMDP (DRDO)

Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme that advanced indigenous missile systems.

National Vision

Promoted long-term development thinking through technology, education, and self-reliance.

Youth Inspiration

Known for interacting with students and encouraging disciplined learning and big dreams.

Public Service

As President, emphasized integrity, accessibility, and a people-first approach to leadership.

Author

Wrote books that connect science, purpose, and national progress for young readers.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam’s legacy is not only in national programs and institutions, but also in the minds he shaped. He showed that excellence can be humble, and that leadership can be rooted in learning.

His message to students remains simple: build skills, stay curious, work with integrity, and use knowledge to serve society.
